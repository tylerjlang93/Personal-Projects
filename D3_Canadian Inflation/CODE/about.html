About Our Visualization Project

Visualization Objective

This work aims to present a domestic and international view of consumer goods inflation in Canada since 1995. Canadian food inflation is contextualized on a global scale by comparing inflation in Canada to other countries. Domestic inflation is explored by presenting the consumer price index (CPI) on a granular scale, looking at individual products, and groups of similar products. Predictions on the future inflation of these groups are also presented. To explore the impact that inflation has had on the Canadian public, reported food insecurity in Canada over the course of the COVID-19 pandemic is evaluated. This work allows end-users to interact with Canadian inflation data in a meaningful way, emphasizing the scale of inflation and its effect on food security, to motivate socially forward data-driven decision-making.

Problem Definition

Recent conflict, climate shocks, and the COVID-19 pandemic have led to rising inflation internationally. Volatile changes in inflation have impacted many different industries and processes from supply chain to food prices and in turn makes it challenging for the government to make effective policies. Understanding changes in food prices is valuable information to inform the public when making decisions on how to create their household budgets and provide for their families in these uncertain times.
